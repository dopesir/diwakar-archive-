He spent a week in Dharavi, not photographing poverty, but industry. 'I saw leather tanners, potters, recyclers — an economy of dignity.'

For a week he followed the work rather than the want — the tanneries, the kilns, the sorting and recycling that fill the day. The series is a portrait of labour, not of lack.
