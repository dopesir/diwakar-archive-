During the locust invasion, Diwakar captured farmers fighting with drums and fire. 'The sky turned dark at noon. A man fell to his knees, not from exhaustion, but from hopelessness.'

The pictures hold both the swarm and the response to it — the noise and smoke raised against the insects, and the standing crop they had come to strip. It is a portrait of a community meeting an old threat with whatever is at hand.
