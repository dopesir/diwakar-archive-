He was one of the first photographers at the blast sites. 'I saw body parts and heard screams. I put my camera down for ten minutes. Some moments are too sacred for a shutter.'

Arriving early, he made the opposite of the expected choice and lowered the camera. The ten minutes he did not photograph became the part of the assignment he remembers most.
